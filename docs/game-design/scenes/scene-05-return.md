## Win Condition A — The Archivist

**Entry requirement:** Player arrives with at least one snapshot.

**Mechanic:** The camera (or its output — a photograph, a polaroid) exists in the player's inventory/world. The player brings it near the father and elders. [TBD: is this a proximity trigger? An object drop? A specific interaction gesture?]

**What happens:** The player discovers (without prompt) that presenting the photo triggers a sharing sequence. Cinematic camera. The father and elders lean in. They look at the photograph. They begin to laugh — not at the photo, but with it, in recognition of the moment it captures.

**The win:** Shared joy. The son has returned with evidence that life had value. The father celebrates without fanfare — simply by laughing. The prodigal is home.

**Ending sequence:** The dead sun — witnessed in Scene 1 — explodes. A supernova. Light expands. Spectacular, warm, final. The world is ending. A new one will begin somewhere. The player and the elders see it together. The game's last image: the son home, the father beside him, the star completing its life.

*Cosmological frame: the universe expands into frozen stillness — this moment is forever preserved in frozen atoms. It was real. It mattered.*

---

## Win Condition B — The Present

**Entry requirement:** Player arrives without snapshots (or does not use them).

**Mechanic:** Somewhere in the scene — on the map, not marked, discoverable by curious players — a physical situation exists that, if encountered, causes the character to lose composure. A stumble. An unexpected sight. A moment of involuntary stillness watching the sky. [TBD: what specifically?]

**What happens:** The character stops. The formal register breaks. They look at something — nature, the sky, the bench, the elders — and begin to laugh. Not at anything. Just laughing. The elders recognize it immediately. They join. The threshold of appreciation is crossed.

**The win:** Same threshold, different path. The son arrived without an archive but found the present moment anyway. The father laughs. The supernova follows.

*What the player learned: you don't need to have curated your life to inhabit it.*

---

## Lose Condition — The Missed Moment (Soft Exit)

**Entry requirement:** Player arrives without snapshots and does not discover Win Condition B.

**What happens:** The elders laugh — they have their own memories, their own present joy. The player watches from outside the circle of laughter. The sun dims. No explosion. The amber disk fades slowly.

**Exit sequence:** A series of snapshot-shaped frames appears — moments from the scenes the player passed through, the photographs they could have taken. Not accusatory. Elegiac. The score is muted. The game ends quietly.

*Cosmological frame: the universe collapses inward and restarts — this cycle renders without meaning. Another will begin. This one was not inhabited.*

**What the player feels:** Not failure. Absence. The son was present but empty-handed. He watched the elders laugh without him. He was there. He just didn't bring anything.

## Scenario

The player arrives back at the hill. The house is the same. The fog is the same. The amber sun is the same.

But the bench is there now, and the father is seated on it with other old people. Waiting. Not anxiously. He has his own life here, his own people. The son is returning to something that continued without him.

The player approaches. What happens next depends on what they carry and what they discover.

**Reference:** "О чём смеются старики? О хороших моментах жизни. И в текущий." — "What do the elders laugh about? About the good moments of life. And the current ones."  
_(SCENARIOS/elders laughing/README.md — original note)_

**Narrative frame:** The father "ran to meet him, and fell on his neck, and kissed him." In this version, the father stays on the bench. But he is waiting. The kiss is the laughter. The feast is whatever the player brings. And if they bring nothing, the feast continues anyway — the son is simply not at the table.

## Design Notes

- This scene is the same location as Scene 1 — the visual continuity is important. The player has returned to where they started. The ring closes.
- The supernova must be spectacular but not frightening. It is not disaster — it is completion. The dead sun, witnessed in Scene 1, finally finishes its life in the presence of the son who came home to see it.
- Win Condition B (the fall into laughter) must be genuinely discoverable — present in the scene's geometry or character behavior, not a dialog option. It should feel like finding something, not choosing something.
- The lose condition exit sequence (missed snapshots slideshow) should be beautiful, not punishing. It is an elegiac acknowledgment, not a failure screen.
